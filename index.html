What are binaural beats?

Binaural beats occur when two slightly different frequencies are played in each ear through headphones. The brain perceives a third frequency — the difference — which guides brainwave activity into specific states: relaxation (alpha 8-13 Hz), deep meditation (theta 4-8 Hz), deep sleep (delta 0.5-4 Hz), active focus (beta 12-30 Hz), or peak cognition (gamma 30-100 Hz).

432 Hz vs 440 Hz — What is the difference?

440 Hz is the modern standard concert pitch (A=440), established internationally in 1939. 432 Hz is an alternative tuning mathematically aligned with the Golden Ratio and Fibonacci sequence, sometimes called "Verdi tuning" or "natural tuning". Many listeners report 432 Hz feels warmer and more natural than 440 Hz. echo.11 offers both — you can compare them directly.
